About Some Rajasthani Jewellery

kundan jewwllery

This style involves setting gemstones, usually diamonds and precious stones, in gold. The stones are typically backed with foil to enhance their brilliance.

Meenakari Jewellery

Known for its intricate enamel work, Meenakari jewellery often features vibrant colors and is crafted by fusing powdered glass onto metal.

Thewa Jewellery

This is a speciality of Rajasthan where intricately designed gold sheets are fused with multicolored glass. Thewa jewellery often depicts scenes from Hindu mythology.

Polki Jewellery

Similar to Kundan, Polki jewellery uses uncut diamonds in their natural form, set in gold or silver, giving it a distinct sparkle .

Jadau Jewellery

This style involves embedding gems and stones into a metal base, often using techniques that date back to Mughal times. It is characterized by its intricate designs and use of precious and semi-precious stones.

Ghunghroo (Anklets)

Anklets adorned with small bells (ghunghroos) are popular among the women of Rajasthan, often worn during traditional dances like Ghoomar.

Borla (Maang Tikka)

A traditional Rajasthani ornament worn on the forehead, usually featuring a large gemstone or a decorative element at its center.

Nath (Nose Ring)

Rajasthan is known for its elaborate nose rings, often oversized and studded with gemstones, worn during weddings and festivals.

Bajubandh (Armlet)

Intricately designed armlets worn around the upper arm, often in silver with intricate carvings or embellishments.
