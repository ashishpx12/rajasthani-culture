About kancha

Kancha, a small village nestled in the foothills of the Himalayas, epitomizes rustic charm and serene beauty. Surrounded by lush greenery and majestic mountains, it offers a tranquil escape from the bustling city life. The village is known for its rich cultural heritage, with traditional music and dance forming an integral part of community celebrations. Agriculture is the backbone of Kancha's economy, with terraced fields yielding a bounty of rice, wheat, and vegetables. The villagers, known for their warm hospitality, welcome visitors with open arms, offering glimpses into their simple yet fulfilling way of life. Ancient temples dot the landscape, reflecting the spiritual essence that permeates daily existence. Kancha's picturesque vistas attract nature enthusiasts and adventurers alike, drawn to its scenic trails and panoramic views. In essence, Kancha embodies the essence of rural India, where tradition meets natural beauty in perfect harmony.

steps to play kancha


1.Gather the materials :- You'll need several small glass or ceramic marbles (kancha),
    a larger marble (gotti), and a flat, sandy or dirt playing surface (like a patch of ground
     or a wooden board with sand).

2.Set up the playing are :- Clear an area and draw a circle on the ground, about 3-4 feet
 in diameter. This will be the playing ring.

3.Decide on the rules :- There are variations of kancha played across different regions.
 Common rules include:
   # First shot - Players take turns shooting marbles into the circle from a predetermined 
   line outside the circle.
   # Hitting and capturing :- A player shoots their marble (kancha) aiming to hit and capture
    other marbles out of the circle.
   # Gotti shot :- Using the larger marble (gotti), players aim to hit marbles (kanchas) out 
   of the circle. If successful, they keep the marbles they hit.

4.Playing the game :-
   - Decide the order of play.
   - Each player shoots their marble (kancha) from outside the circle towards the marbles 
   inside.
   - The objective is to hit the marbles (kanchas) in the circle.
   - If a player hits a marble out of the circle, they get to keep it.
   - If a player misses or fouls (e.g., touching the line with their hand), their turn ends.

5.Winning the game :- The game typically ends when all marbles (kanchas) have been 
captured from the circle. The winner is the player who has captured the most marbles.

6.Additional rules :- Depending on local customs or agreements, there may be additional 
rules governing how marbles are shot, retrieved, or handled during play.
